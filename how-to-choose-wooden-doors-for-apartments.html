How to Choose Wooden Doors for Apartments in India

By Motion Woods, Pune March 2025 8 min read

Choosing the right wooden door for an apartment is one of the most important decisions in a residential project — whether you are a homebuyer fitting out your new flat, or a builder specifying doors across hundreds of units. The wrong choice can mean frequent repairs, poor finish quality, or unnecessary cost.

At Motion Woods, we have manufactured and installed over 7,650 doors across Pune's top residential projects — for builders like VTP Realty, Yashada Realty, Pride Purple Group, and Roshan Builders. This guide shares exactly what we tell every project manager and homeowner who asks us: how to choose the right wooden door for an apartment.

"The right door is not the most expensive one — it is the one that matches the location, usage, and finish level of that specific room."

Step 1 — Understand the Location of Each Door

The single most important factor in choosing a door is where it will be used. Different rooms have completely different requirements:

  • Main entrance door — needs strength, security, and a premium look. This is the first impression of the home.
  • Bedroom doors — need sound insulation, smooth finish, and durability. Moderate budget range.
  • Bathroom & toilet doors — must be moisture resistant and waterproof. Regular wood will warp and rot.
  • Kitchen doors — need to handle grease, heat, and frequent use. Laminate finish is preferred.
  • Utility & store room doors — basic flush doors with minimal finish work fine here.

Most apartments have 6 to 10 doors — and each one has a different requirement. Specifying the same door type for every room is the most common and costly mistake we see.

Step 2 — Know Your Door Types

Flush Doors

The most widely used door type in Indian residential apartments. A flush door has a flat, smooth surface on both sides — made from a timber frame filled with a core material (solid wood, honeycomb, or particle board) and covered with plywood or MDF skin.

  • Best for: bedroom doors, living room doors, office doors
  • Available finishes: laminate, veneer, paint-ready
  • Price range: budget to mid-range
  • Lifespan: 10–15 years with proper maintenance

WPC Doors (Wood Plastic Composite)

WPC doors are made from a composite of wood fibre and plastic polymer — making them completely waterproof and termite-proof. These are the ideal choice for bathrooms, toilets, and utility areas in Indian apartments where humidity is high.

  • Best for: bathrooms, toilets, utility rooms, basement doors
  • Completely waterproof — will not warp, swell, or rot
  • Termite and borer proof
  • Low maintenance — wipe clean with a damp cloth
  • Price range: mid-range, slightly higher than standard flush doors

Veneer Doors

Veneer doors are flush doors with a thin layer of natural wood (veneer) applied to the surface — giving a premium, natural wood grain appearance at a lower cost than solid wood. These are commonly used for master bedroom doors and main bedroom entries in premium residential projects.

  • Best for: master bedroom, living room, premium residential
  • Natural wood grain appearance
  • Finished with PU polish — matte or gloss
  • Price range: mid to premium

Solid Teakwood Doors

Solid teakwood doors are the premium choice for main entrance doors in bungalows, villas, and luxury apartments. Teak is naturally oil-rich, making it resistant to warping, termites, and moisture. A well-made teakwood door with quality hardware and polish will last 30–50 years.

  • Best for: main entrance doors, bungalows, luxury villas
  • Natural teak grain — polished with PU or Duco finish
  • CNC carving and custom designs available
  • Price range: premium
  • Lifespan: 30–50 years with periodic polish

Step 3 — Choose the Right Finish

The finish on a door determines how it looks, how easy it is to maintain, and how long it lasts. These are the main finish options used in Indian residential apartments:

Finish Type Look Best For Maintenance
Laminate Smooth, solid colour or wood-grain print Bedrooms, kitchen, utility Low
PU Polish Natural wood grain, matte or gloss Main door, master bedroom Medium
Duco Paint Solid colour, very smooth Premium interiors, accent doors Medium
Melamine Semi-gloss, natural look Bedroom, wardrobe shutters Low

Step 4 — Get the Size Right

Standard apartment door sizes in India are as follows — but always measure your opening before ordering, as sizes vary between builders and projects:

  • Main entrance door: 4 feet × 8 feet (1200mm × 2100mm) — standard, or custom wider for premium projects
  • Bedroom doors: 3 feet × 7 feet (900mm × 2100mm) — most common
  • Bathroom doors: 2.5 feet × 7 feet (750mm × 2100mm) — standard
  • Utility/store room: 2.5 feet × 7 feet (750mm × 2100mm)

Always confirm door frame thickness with your carpenter before ordering — standard frame thickness is 4 inches for a 9-inch wall and 5 inches for a 12-inch wall.

Step 5 — Choose the Right Hardware

Hardware is the part most homeowners ignore — and then regret. A beautiful door with poor quality hinges, handles, or locks will feel cheap and break within a year. These are the minimum hardware specs we recommend:

  • Hinges: stainless steel, minimum 4-inch for bedroom doors, 5-inch for main doors. 3 hinges per door.
  • Handles & locks: branded hardware — Godrej, Dorset, or equivalent. Avoid unbranded imported hardware.
  • Door stopper: floor-mounted or wall-mounted — prevents wall damage from door handle impact.
  • Hydraulic door closer: recommended for main entrance doors — self-closing, prevents slamming.
